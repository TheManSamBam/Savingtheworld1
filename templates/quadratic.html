QUADRATIC EQUATIONS

A quadratic equation is a second-degree polynomial equation of a single variable, typically in the form of: ax^2 + bx + c = 0 where "a," "b," and "c" are coefficients that determine the shape and position of the equation's graph, and "x" is the variable. The highest power of the variable "x" in a quadratic equation is two, which gives the equation its name. Quadratic equations can have two, one, or zero real solutions depending on the values of the coefficients. The solutions of a quadratic equation can be found using the quadratic formula or by factoring the equation. Quadratic equations are commonly used in physics, engineering, economics, and many other fields to model a wide range of phenomena.
